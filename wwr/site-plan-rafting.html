Overview

Purpose

The purpose of this website is to inspire adventure seekers to experience the thrill of whitewater rafting with our company. By providing clear information about our guided trips, safety standards, booking options, and stunning local rivers, our goal is to attract new customers, build trust with returning guests, and make the reservation process easy and convenient. This website will help us reach a wider audience, showcase our unique river experiences, and grow our business by turning website visitors into loyal rafters.

Audience

Our target audience is adventure-loving individuals, families, and groups—typically ages 18–50—who are looking for an exciting outdoor experience. Many are first-time or occasional rafters who value safety, clear trip details, and trustworthy guides. Some visitors will be seasoned outdoor enthusiasts searching for challenging rapids and unique destinations. As we build this site, we will keep this audience in mind by using energetic, bold visuals, high-quality river photos, and colors that evoke nature and excitement—like blues, greens, and splashes of white. Our fonts will be clean and easy to read but convey a sense of adventure. The content will be welcoming, informative, and straightforward, helping visitors quickly find trip options, safety information, and easy online booking so they feel confident choosing our company for their next river adventure.

FAQ Page

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a good trip for kids? What are the age ranges you allow?

Absolutely this is a good trip for children of all ages. We have found however that children over the age of 8 seem to enjoy it more overall. A common concern we get from parents is that their children will be bored stuck on a boat. Surprisingly, we deal with very few bored children. The scenery, wildlife, and rapids keep things exciting. Plus, each boat has a guide who loves to tell stories!

Is whitewater rafting safe? I don't even know how to swim! Can I still come?

There is inherent risk with all adventure sports, but we mitigate this with professional guides who are certified in Wilderness First Aid. All guests wear Coast Guard approved life vests. Many non-swimmers join and have a great time. A safety talk is given before every trip.

What is included in the trip?

We provide all camping equipment (sleeping bag, pad, tent, cot, duffle bags, utensils, camping chair), drinks (water, lemonade, coffee, tea), and meals/snacks. Alcohol and soda can be brought in reasonable amounts (2 cans per day).

What do I need to bring?

Be prepared for both hot and cold conditions. Bring rain gear (not ponchos), quick-dry clothing, sun protection (hat, sunglasses), sandals/hiking shoes, personal hygiene items, flashlight, water bottle, and optional comfort items like books or cameras.

What is so great about whitewater rafting anyway?

From thrilling rapids to scenic beauty and relaxing camp life, every day is a memorable adventure. Enjoy fresh meals, stunning views, hikes, and storytelling by the river under a starry sky. Don't miss the dutch oven brownies!

How far in advance should I book my trip?

If you have specific dates, book a year in advance. For flexible dates, 6 months is usually enough.
